Platypus

Together with the four species of echidna, it is one of the five extant species of monotremes, the only mammals that lay eggs instead of giving birth.

  • Scientific Name: Ornithorhynchus anatinus
  • Average Length: 50 centimeters
  • Average Lifespan: 11 years
  • Habitat: Small streams and rivers

The platypus, also known as the duck-billed platypus, is a semiaquatic egg-laying mammal endemic to eastern Australia, including Tasmania. The animal is the sole living representative of its family (Ornithorhynchidae) and genus (Ornithorhynchus), though a number of related species have been found in the fossil record.
